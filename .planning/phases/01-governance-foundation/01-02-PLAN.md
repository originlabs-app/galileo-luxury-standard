  <action>
Create governance/CHARTER.md following the Hyperledger Foundation Charter structure from research.

Structure the charter with these sections:

1. **Mission and Scope**
   - Mission: Establish interoperable digital product identity standard for luxury goods
   - Core value: "Prot√©ger le patrimoine des marques et le savoir-faire humain"
   - Scope: Specification development, reference implementations, certification program

2. **Membership Categories** (from CONTEXT.md decisions)
   - Founding Partners: Time-limited enrollment window (closes at charter ratification), FTE commitment for TSC
   - Members: Paid tier, voting rights, revenue-scaled dues (SME/Mid/Enterprise bands)
   - Observers: Free access, RFC participation, no voting rights, commercial use under Apache 2.0
   - Reference: Full details in membership/MEMBERSHIP_LEVELS.md

3. **Governing Board** (strategic decisions)
   - Composition: Representatives from Member and Founding Partner organizations
   - Responsibilities: Budget, strategy, membership policies, trademark
   - Voting: Majority for standard decisions, 2/3 for charter amendments

4. **Technical Steering Committee (TSC)** (technical decisions - from CONTEXT.md)
   - Composition: 11 members (hybrid Hyperledger model)
     - 6 elected by Active Contributors (meritocratic)
     - 3 appointed by Governing Board (expertise/diversity)
     - 2 from Founding Partners (transitional, expires after 3 years)
   - Active Contributor definition: Accepted contribution in prior 12 months
   - Term limits: 2-year terms, renewable once, 1-year gap before re-election
   - Responsibilities: Specification approval, RFC decisions, technical roadmap
   - Reference: Detailed procedures in tsc/ directory

5. **Voting Procedures**
   - Quorum: 2/3 of voting members
   - Standard decisions: Majority of members present
   - Breaking changes: Unanimous consent with veto rights (per CONTEXT.md)
   - Electronic voting: Majority of all members (not just those voting)

6. **Veto Mechanism for Breaking Changes** (critical - from CONTEXT.md)
   - Definition: Changes that make conformant implementations non-conformant
   - Veto rights: Any TSC member can veto breaking changes
   - Requirements: Written declaration, technical justification, alternative proposal
   - Override: Unanimous TSC consent OR 2/3 Governing Board vote
   - Resolution period: 90 days for negotiation

7. **Intellectual Property Policy**
   - License: Apache License 2.0 (reference governance/LICENSE)
   - Contributions: DCO sign-off required (reference governance/DCO.md)
   - Patent grant: Per Apache 2.0 Section 3
   - Trademarks: Held by Foundation, usage policy TBD

8. **Transparency Policy** (from CONTEXT.md - hybrid model)
   - Deliberations: Private (protects competitive concerns between brands)
   - Decisions: Published with full rationale
   - Meeting minutes: Summary published, detailed minutes for members
   - Decision log: Public, immutable

9. **Antitrust Compliance**
   - Safe harbor language for competitor collaboration
   - Prohibited topics: Pricing, market allocation, customer restrictions
   - Legal counsel availability for sensitive discussions

10. **Code of Conduct**
    - Reference to governance/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md
    - Applies to all participants in all venues

11. **Amendments**
    - Process: RFC submitted, 60-day review, 2/3 Board vote
    - Effective date: 30 days after approval

Include a "Version History" section at the end (initially: v1.0.0-draft).

Use formal but accessible language. Reference Ostrom's commons principles implicitly through the structure (clear boundaries, participatory decision-making, graduated sanctions, conflict resolution).

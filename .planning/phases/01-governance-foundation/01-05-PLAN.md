  <name>Task 2: Create election procedures</name>
  <files>governance/tsc/ELECTIONS.md</files>
  <action>
Create governance/tsc/ELECTIONS.md documenting the election process for TSC seats.

Structure:

**TSC Election Procedures**

1. **Election Schedule**
   - Annual elections for elected seats
   - Staggered terms: ~3 seats elected each year (of the 6 elected seats)
   - Election timeline: Nomination (2 weeks) -> Campaign (2 weeks) -> Voting (1 week) -> Announcement

2. **Eligibility**
   - To run: Must be Active Contributor (contribution in prior 12 months)
   - To vote: Must be Active Contributor at election start date
   - Employees of same organization: Max 2 TSC seats total (prevents single-org dominance per CONTEXT.md)

3. **Nomination Process**
   - Self-nomination or nomination by another Active Contributor
   - Nomination requires: Candidate statement (500 words max), endorsement by 2 other Active Contributors
   - Candidate statement includes: Background, vision for standard, time commitment

4. **Campaign Period**
   - Candidates may participate in Q&A sessions
   - Campaign on mailing list, not external channels (level playing field)
   - No corporate promotion or paid advertising

5. **Voting**
   - Method: Condorcet voting (ranked choice) via [system TBD]
   - One vote per Active Contributor
   - Secret ballot
   - Results verified by independent party (not running for election)

6. **Appointed Seats**
   - Governing Board appoints 3 seats for expertise/diversity
   - Appointment criteria: Fill gaps in TSC expertise, geographic diversity, industry segment diversity
   - Appointed members serve same term length as elected members

7. **Founding Partner Seats (Transitional)**
   - 2 seats reserved for founding brands
   - Selection: Founding Partners jointly nominate
   - Sunset: These seats convert to elected seats after [3 years from charter ratification]

8. **Term Limits** (per CHARTER.md)
   - Term length: 2 years
   - Maximum consecutive terms: 2
   - Gap requirement: 1 year before re-eligibility after serving 2 consecutive terms

9. **Vacancies**
   - Mid-term vacancy: Special election within 60 days
   - If <6 months remaining in term: Appointed by remaining TSC until next regular election

10. **Removal**
    - Grounds: Violation of Code of Conduct, prolonged absence (3 consecutive meetings), conflict of interest
    - Process: Motion by TSC member, 2/3 vote to remove
    - Right to respond: Member has opportunity to address allegations before vote

Reference CHARTER.md for the authoritative TSC structure definition.

Create governance/tsc/MEETINGS.md documenting TSC meeting operations.

Structure:

**TSC Meeting Operations**

1. **Meeting Cadence**
   - Regular meetings: Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks)
   - Duration: 60-90 minutes
   - Schedule: Published quarterly in advance
   - Time rotation: Alternate between time zones (EU-friendly, APAC-friendly, Americas-friendly)

2. **Meeting Types**
   - Regular TSC: Standing agenda, RFC reviews, roadmap updates
   - Special session: Called for urgent matters (security issues, breaking RFC)
   - Working group: Focused topic, open to non-TSC participants

3. **Attendance**
   - TSC members: Expected to attend, notify if absent
   - Quorum: 2/3 of TSC members (8 of 11) per CHARTER.md
   - Observers: Members and Observers may attend public sessions
   - Executive session: TSC members only (for personnel, legal matters)

4. **Transparency Policy** (per CONTEXT.md - hybrid model)
   - Deliberations: May be private (protects competitive concerns)
   - Decisions: Always published with rationale
   - Meeting minutes:
     - Summary minutes: Public (actions, decisions, attendees)
     - Detailed minutes: Available to Members and Founding Partners
   - Recordings: Not published (encourages candid discussion)

5. **Agenda**
   - Published 3 business days before meeting
   - Standing items: Previous action items, RFC status, upcoming releases
   - Agenda requests: Any participant may request item via [process TBD]
   - Chair sets final agenda

6. **Decision Making**
   - Default: Lazy consensus (no objections = approved)
   - Explicit vote: When consensus unclear or member requests
   - Voting: Show of hands (or roll call for remote)
   - Tie: Chair breaks tie
   - Breaking changes: Veto rules per CHARTER.md apply

7. **Minutes Template**
   - Date, time, attendees (with member/observer distinction)
   - Agenda items discussed
   - Decisions made (with brief rationale)
   - Action items (owner, deadline)
   - Next meeting date

8. **Minutes Location**
   - Published to: governance/tsc/minutes/YYYY-MM-DD.md
   - Archive: All minutes preserved indefinitely

9. **Remote Participation**
   - Primary: Video conference (platform TBD)
   - Backup: Dial-in audio
   - Chat: For questions during meeting
   - Async: For those who cannot attend live, review recordings/minutes

10. **Emergency Procedures**
    - Security issues: Chair may call emergency session with 24h notice
    - Quorum exception: For security-only sessions, 6 of 11 sufficient
    - Out-of-band decisions: May be ratified electronically, confirmed at next regular meeting

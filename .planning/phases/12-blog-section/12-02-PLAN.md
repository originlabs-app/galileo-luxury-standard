Today marks a major milestone: **Galileo Luxury Standard v1.0.0 is production-ready**. After months of careful development, we're shipping a complete, open standard for luxury product authentication, regulatory compliance, and provenance tracking.

## The Problem We're Solving

The luxury industry faces three critical challenges:

- **Counterfeiting costs $81B+ annually** — fake products undermine brand value and consumer trust
- **ESPR mandates Digital Product Passports by 2027** — brands need compliant solutions now
- **Existing solutions are proprietary silos** — platforms don't interoperate, creating vendor lock-in
- **Regulatory complexity is increasing** — GDPR, MiCA, and ESPR require specialized compliance

Galileo exists to solve these problems with an **open, neutral standard** that any brand or technology provider can adopt without sacrifice or dependency.

## What's Included in v1.0.0

### Governance & Standards (8 specifications)

- **Technical Steering Committee charter** with anti-dominance rules (max 2 seats per organization)
- **RFC contribution process** enabling community-driven evolution
- **Apache 2.0 license** ensuring the standard remains open and non-capturable
- **Semantic versioning policy** guaranteeing backward compatibility

### Core Data Models (9 specifications)

- **ESPR-compliant Digital Product Passport schema** with mandatory fields (GTIN, material composition, carbon footprint, repair instructions)
- **EPCIS 2.0 lifecycle events** covering manufacture, commission, first sale, repair/MRO, resale, and decommission
- **Provenance grading system** with 5 grades (Verified, Attested, Self-Declared, Unverified, None)
- **Molecular signature extensions** for ultra-luxury provenance verification (terroir, materials)

### Identity Infrastructure (7 specifications)

- **DID method for products and organizations** following W3C Decentralized Identifiers specification
- **ONCHAINID integration** for KYC/KYB compliance using ERC-734/735 claim-based identity
- **W3C Verifiable Credentials** enabling privacy-preserving attestations
- **Trusted Issuers Registry** for managing authorized claim issuers

### Token & Compliance (8 specifications)

- **ERC-3643 extension** for regulated luxury product tokens with single-supply pattern (1 token = 1 physical product)
- **Modular compliance system** with pluggable rules (jurisdiction restrictions, balance limits, time locks)
- **MiCA-compliant transfer validation** including Travel Rule (VASP identification)
- **ERC-4337 account abstraction** enabling gasless transactions for improved UX

### Infrastructure & Resolver (6 specifications)

- **GS1 Digital Link resolver** transforming physical identifiers (GTIN + serial) into resolvable URLs
- **Context-aware resolution** delivering role-appropriate views (consumer vs. brand vs. regulator)
- **RBAC framework** with 7 role types for access control
- **Audit trail specification** with 7-year retention for regulatory compliance

**Total: 38 specifications, 17 Solidity interfaces, 17 JSON schemas**

## What You Can Build

### Luxury Brands
Implement product certificates with regulatory compliance built-in. No vendor lock-in, no proprietary APIs. Own your data, control your customer relationships.

### Technology Providers
Build solutions on an open standard. Compete on implementation quality, not platform control. Interoperability guaranteed.

### Integrators
Connect existing ERP/PLM systems to blockchain provenance using standard schemas. Plug into any compliant solution.

### Researchers
Study real-world blockchain applications in luxury with complete specification transparency.

## What's NOT in v1.0 (Yet)

Being honest about current scope:

- **Smart contract reference implementations** → Planned for v1.1 (Q2 2026)
- **Multi-chain support** → Ethereum-first, expanding to Polygon/Arbitrum in v1.2
- **Consumer mobile apps** → Specification only, not implementation
- **Localization (i18n)** → English-first for v1.0

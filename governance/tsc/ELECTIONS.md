## 1. Election Schedule

The TSC conducts annual elections for elected seats to ensure orderly transitions and continuity of governance.

### 1.1 Annual Cycle

| Phase | Timing | Duration |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Nomination Period | September 1-14 | 2 weeks |
| Campaign Period | September 15-28 | 2 weeks |
| Voting Period | October 1-7 | 1 week |
| Results Announcement | October 8-14 | Within 7 days |
| New Terms Begin | January 1 | - |

### 1.2 Staggered Terms

To ensure governance continuity, elected seats are staggered:
- Approximately three (3) seats elected each year (of the 6 elected seats)
- No more than half the elected seats change in any single election
- First election may elect all 6 seats with terms of 1 or 2 years to establish staggering

### 1.3 Election Calendar Publication

The TSC Chair publishes the election calendar by August 1 each year, confirming:
- Exact dates for each phase
- Active Contributor roster snapshot date (30 days before nomination opens)
- Election officials for the year

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Candidate Eligibility

To run for an elected TSC seat, an individual must:

1. **Be an Active Contributor:** Have a contribution accepted during the prior twelve (12) months as defined in [MEMBERS.md](./MEMBERS.md) Section 4
2. **Agree to Time Commitment:** Commit to attending 75% of regular TSC meetings
3. **Disclose Affiliations:** Provide current employer/organizational affiliation
4. **Accept Term Obligations:** Agree to serve the full 2-year term if elected

### 2.2 Voter Eligibility

To vote in TSC elections, an individual must:

1. **Be an Active Contributor** at the election start date (roster snapshot)
2. **Not be employed by an organization already at the 2-seat limit** (see Section 2.3)

### 2.3 Anti-Dominance Provision

**Maximum 2 TSC seats per organization** prevents single-organization dominance.

**Application to elections:**
- Candidates from organizations with 2 existing TSC members are ineligible
- If a candidate's organization reaches 2 seats during counting, excess candidate(s) are skipped
- "Organization" includes parent companies, wholly-owned subsidiaries, and affiliates

**Determining organization:**
- Primary employment relationship at time of election
- Self-employed individuals count as their own organization
- Consultants count toward their employer, not their clients

## 3. Nomination Process

### 3.1 Types of Nomination

| Type | Description | Requirements |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| Self-nomination | Candidate nominates themselves | Candidate statement + 2 endorsements |
| Peer nomination | Another Active Contributor nominates | Nominee consent + candidate statement + 2 endorsements |

### 3.2 Nomination Requirements

A valid nomination includes:

1. **Candidate Statement** (500 words maximum)
   - Background and relevant experience
   - Vision for the Standard's technical direction
   - Time commitment availability
   - Areas of expertise to contribute

2. **Endorsements**
   - Two (2) endorsements from other Active Contributors
   - Endorsers may not be from the same organization as the candidate
   - Endorsements submitted via [nomination system TBD]

3. **Disclosures**
   - Current employer/organization
   - Any potential conflicts of interest
   - Previous TSC service (for term limit verification)

### 3.3 Nomination Submission

Nominations are submitted to:
- **Email:** elections@galileoprotocol.io
- **System:** [Election platform TBD]
- **Deadline:** End of day (23:59 UTC) on the final day of nomination period

### 3.4 Nomination Verification

The Election Committee verifies:
- Candidate is an Active Contributor
- Organization limit not exceeded
- Statement within word limit
- Two valid endorsements received
- No term limit conflicts

Invalid nominations are returned to the candidate with explanation and opportunity to cure (if time permits).

## 4. Campaign Period

The campaign period allows candidates to present their qualifications and vision to voters.

### 4.1 Campaign Activities

**Permitted:**
- Participation in organized Q&A sessions
- Responses to questions on official mailing list
- Candidate statements published on project website
- Attendance at public working group meetings

**Not Permitted:**
- Campaign activities on external social media or channels
- Corporate promotion or employer-sponsored advertising
- Paid advertising of any kind
- Soliciting endorsements during voting period
- Negative campaigning against other candidates

### 4.2 Level Playing Field

To ensure fairness:
- All candidates receive equal placement on ballot
- Candidate statements are formatted identically
- Q&A sessions allocate equal time per candidate
- No organization may provide resources for individual campaigns

### 4.3 Q&A Sessions

- At least one (1) community Q&A session held during campaign period
- Sessions recorded and published for asynchronous viewing
- Questions submitted in advance and selected by Election Committee
- Each candidate receives equal time for responses

## 5. Voting

### 5.1 Voting Method

**Condorcet voting (ranked-choice)** is used for TSC elections.

- Voters rank candidates in order of preference
- Condorcet winner (candidate who beats all others in pairwise comparison) is selected
- If no Condorcet winner exists, Schulze method determines winner
- Ties resolved by random selection (witnessed by Election Committee)

### 5.2 Ballot

Voters receive a ballot listing all verified candidates in randomized order. Voters may:
- Rank all candidates
- Rank only some candidates (unranked = tied for last)
- Abstain (not submit a ballot)

### 5.3 Voting Rules

- **One vote per Active Contributor**
- **Secret ballot:** Individual votes are never disclosed
- **No proxy voting:** Votes cannot be delegated
- **Voting period:** 7 days (October 1-7)
- **Late votes:** Not accepted after deadline

### 5.4 Voting Platform

Voting is conducted via [platform TBD] with:
- Voter authentication via Active Contributor roster
- Encrypted ballot submission
- Audit trail for vote integrity
- Results calculation by Election Committee

## 6. Results and Verification

### 6.1 Vote Counting

- Counting conducted by Election Committee within 3 days of voting close
- Condorcet/Schulze algorithm applied to ranked ballots
- Anti-dominance rule applied during counting (skip candidates from over-limit organizations)

### 6.2 Results Verification

- Results verified by independent party (not running for election, not on Election Committee)
- Anonymized ballots preserved for 90 days for audit
- Election algorithm is deterministic and reproducible

### 6.3 Results Publication

Within seven (7) days of voting close, publish:
- Elected candidates with vote counts
- Total ballots cast and participation rate
- Condorcet matrix (pairwise comparison results)
- Any anti-dominance rule applications

### 6.4 Challenges

Election results may be challenged within 14 days of publication by:
- Any candidate
- Any Active Contributor with specific evidence of irregularity

Challenges reviewed by Governing Board, whose decision is final.

## 7. Appointed Seats

Three (3) TSC seats are appointed by the Governing Board per [CHARTER.md](../CHARTER.md) Section 4.1.

### 7.1 Purpose

Appointed seats ensure the TSC has:
- Expertise not represented by elected members
- Geographic diversity across luxury market regions
- Industry segment diversity (haute couture, horlogerie, joaillerie, maroquinerie, etc.)
- External perspective from standards bodies, academia, or adjacent industries

### 7.2 Appointment Process

1. **Gap Analysis:** TSC Chair provides Board with expertise/diversity gaps after election results
2. **Nomination:** Board members may nominate candidates
3. **Review:** Nominees evaluated against gap analysis criteria
4. **Selection:** Board votes on appointments (majority required)
5. **Acceptance:** Appointed members accept in writing

### 7.3 Appointment Timing

- Appointments made within 60 days of election results
- Terms begin January 1 (same as elected seats)
- Appointed members serve same 2-year terms as elected members

### 7.4 Anti-Dominance Application

The 2-seat-per-organization limit applies equally to appointed seats. Board may not appoint members whose organization already holds 2 TSC seats.

## 8. Founding Partner Seats (Transitional)

Two (2) TSC seats are reserved for Founding Partner organizations as a transitional provision.

### 8.1 Selection

- Founding Partners jointly nominate representatives
- Nominations require consensus among Founding Partners
- If consensus cannot be reached, seats rotate among Founding Partners annually

### 8.2 Transitional Nature

These seats are explicitly transitional:
- **Sunset:** Expire three (3) years from Charter ratification date
- **Conversion:** Upon expiration, convert to elected seats (increasing elected seats to 8)
- **Transition period:** Announced 12 months before expiration

## 9. Term Limits

Per [CHARTER.md](../CHARTER.md) Section 4.4:

### 9.1 Term Length

- Standard term: Two (2) years
- Terms run January 1 to December 31

### 9.2 Consecutive Term Limit

- Maximum: Two (2) consecutive terms
- After two consecutive terms, member must observe a one (1) year gap
- Gap applies to both elected and appointed seats

### 9.3 Re-eligibility

| Situation | Re-eligible |
|-----------|-------------|
| Completed 1 term, did not run | Immediately |
| Completed 2 consecutive terms | After 1 year gap |
| Resigned mid-term | Immediately (term counts if served >1 year) |
| Removed for cause | Subject to Governing Board determination |

### 9.4 Term Limit Enforcement

- Election Committee verifies term history during nomination verification
- Ineligible candidates are notified and nomination rejected
- Historical term records maintained in [MEMBERS.md](./MEMBERS.md)

## 10. Vacancies

### 10.1 Mid-Term Vacancy Causes

- Resignation
- Removal for cause (per Code of Conduct)
- Extended absence (3 consecutive meetings without notice)
- Death or incapacity
- Employment change violating anti-dominance provision

### 10.2 Vacancy Procedures

| Remaining Term | Procedure |
|----------------|-----------|
| 6+ months | Special election within 60 days |
| <6 months | TSC appoints interim member until next regular election |

### 10.3 Special Elections

Special elections follow the same process as regular elections with compressed timeline:
- Nomination: 1 week
- Campaign: 1 week
- Voting: 5 days
- Results: 3 days

### 10.4 Interim Appointments

For vacancies with <6 months remaining:
- Remaining TSC members appoint by majority vote
- Appointee must meet Active Contributor requirements
- Appointee serves until next regular election
- Service as interim appointee does not count toward term limits

## 11. Removal

### 11.1 Grounds for Removal

TSC members may be removed for:
- Violation of Code of Conduct
- Prolonged absence (3 consecutive meetings without valid excuse)
- Conflict of interest not disclosed or not remediated
- Breach of antitrust compliance obligations
- Failure to maintain Active Contributor status (elected members)

### 11.2 Removal Process

1. **Motion:** Any TSC member may motion for removal, stating specific grounds
2. **Notice:** Subject member notified with at least 14 days to respond
3. **Response:** Subject member may provide written response and/or address TSC
4. **Vote:** Two-thirds (2/3) of TSC members (excluding subject) must vote to remove
5. **Effective:** Removal effective immediately upon vote

### 11.3 Appeals

Removed members may appeal to the Governing Board within 30 days. The Board may:
- Affirm removal
- Reinstate the member
- Modify the outcome (e.g., suspension instead of removal)

Board decision is final.

## 12. Election Committee

### 12.1 Composition

The Election Committee consists of:
- TSC Chair (or designee if Chair is running)
- One (1) Governing Board member
- One (1) Active Contributor not running for election

### 12.2 Responsibilities

- Publish election calendar
- Verify nominations and eligibility
- Administer voting platform
- Count votes and certify results
- Respond to candidate and voter inquiries
- Address challenges and irregularities

### 12.3 Conflicts of Interest

Election Committee members who become candidates must recuse themselves. Alternate members are designated before each election.
